Mental Health Disorders

Anxiety Disorders.

Anxiety disorder: Excessive, persistent worry or fear affecting daily life; treatable with therapy and/or medication.

Mood Disorder

Mood disorder: Disturbances in emotions, such as depression or bipolar disorder, impacting a person's overall mood and well-being. Treatment may involve therapy and medication.

psychotic Disorders

Psychotic disorders: Severe mental conditions involving a loss of touch with reality, marked by hallucinations, delusions, and impaired thinking. Treatment often includes medication and therapy.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorder: Disrupted eating patterns leading to physical and mental health issues; common types include anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder. Treatment involves therapy and nutritional support.

personality Disorders

Personality disorders: Persistent and inflexible patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that deviate from cultural expectations, causing interpersonal and functional difficulties. Treatment may involve therapy, but can be challenging due to the enduring nature of the patterns..

Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Neurodevelopmental disorders: Conditions affecting the development of the nervous system, leading to behavioral, cognitive, or social challenges. Examples include autism spectrum disorder and attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD). Treatment often involves behavioral interventions and support.

Dissociative Disorders

Dissociative disorders: Mental health conditions involving a disconnection between thoughts, identity, consciousness, and memory. Treatment may include psychotherapy to address underlying trauma.

Trauma and Stressor realted Disorders

Trauma and Stressor-Related Disorders: Mental health conditions resulting from exposure to traumatic or stressful events, such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Treatment involves therapy and support to address the impact of the experiences.

sleep Disorders

Sleep disorders: Conditions affecting the quality, timing, and duration of sleep, leading to disturbances in the sleep-wake cycle. Examples include insomnia and sleep apnea. Treatment varies and may include lifestyle changes, therapy, or medication.

Substance Related and Addictive Disorders

Substance-Related and Addictive Disorders: Conditions characterized by problematic use of substances, consequences. Treatment involves counseling, support groups, and sometimes medication.

Neurocognitive Disorders

Neurocognitive disorders: Conditions involving cognitive decline, affecting memory, thinking, and daily functioning. Examples include Alzheimer's disease. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and providing support.

somatoform Disorders

Somatoform Disorders: Conditions where physical symptoms are present without a clear medical explanation, often linked to psychological distress. Treatment typically involves psychotherapy to address underlying emotional issues.
