About psychotic

Psychotic disorders are a group of mental health conditions characterized by the presence of psychosis, which involves a significant impairment in an individual's thinking, emotions, and ability to perceive reality. People with psychotic disorders may experience delusions, hallucinations, disorganized thinking, and impaired insight.

Schizophrenia: This is a chronic and severe mental disorder that affects a person's thinking, feeling, and behavior. Individuals with schizophrenia may experience hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, and social withdrawal. Schizoaffective Disorder: This disorder combines symptoms of schizophrenia with mood disturbances, such as depression or mania.

Bipolar Disorder with Psychotic Features: Individuals with bipolar disorder may experience episodes of mania or hypomania, and in some cases, psychosis can occur during these mood episodes. Delusional Disorder: This involves persistent false beliefs (delusions) that are not accompanied by other psychotic symptoms.

Brief Psychotic Disorder: This is a short-term, acute episode of psychosis that may be triggered by stress or trauma. It typically lasts for less than a month. Substance-Induced Psychotic Disorder: Psychosis can be induced by the use of certain substances, such as drugs or alcohol.

Symptoms

Hallucinations Symptoms

Perceiving things that are not present. Hallucinations can involve seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting, or feeling things that others do not..

Delusions Symptoms

Strongly held false beliefs that are resistant to reason or contrary to reality. Delusions can be paranoid (believing others are plotting against them), grandiose (having an exaggerated sense of self-importance), or bizarre in nature.

Disorganized Thinking Symptoms

A disruption in thought processes that can manifest as incoherent speech, difficulty organizing thoughts, or jumping between unrelated topics.

Negative symptoms

These involve a decrease in or loss of normal functioning. Negative symptoms can include reduced emotional expression, social withdrawal, and a lack of motivation.
