About Mood disorder

A mood disorder is a mental health condition characterized by significant and persistent disturbances in a person's emotional state or mood. These disorders can affect a person's overall emotional well-being, energy levels, and ability to function in daily life. There are several types of mood disorders, with the two main categories being depressive disorders and bipolar disorders.

Mood disorders can significantly impact various aspects of a person's life, including relationships, work, and overall quality of life. The exact causes of mood disorders are not fully understood, but factors such as genetics, brain chemistry, and life experiences may contribute to their development.

Treatment typically involves a combination of psychotherapy, medication, and lifestyle changes. It's crucial for individuals experiencing symptoms of a mood disorder to seek professional help for an accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment.


Mood disorder - Types
  • Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
  • Bipolar Disorder (BD)
  • Cyclothymic Disorder

Symptoms

Depressive Symptoms

Depressive symptoms of mood disorder include Persistent Sadness, muscle tension, Loss of Interest or Pleasure, Changes in Sleep Patterns, Changes in Appetite or Weight, Fatigue, Feelings of Worthlessness or Guilt,Difficulty Concentrating, Suicidal Thoughts.

Manic/Hypomanic Symptoms

Manic/Hypomanic symptoms of Mood disorder Elevated Mood, irritability, Increased Energy, Racing Thoughts,Decreased Need for Sleep,Talkativeness, and a Increased Goal-Directed Activity.

Remedies

  • Healthy Diet
  • Regular Exercises
  • Adequate Sleep
  • Stress Management
  • Social Support
  • Limit Alcohol and Caffeine
  • Mindfulness and Relaxation Techniques
  • Sunlight Exposure
  • Establish a Routine
  • Limit Screen Time
