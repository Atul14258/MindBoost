About somatoform

Somatoform disorders, now categorized under the broader term "somatic symptom and related disorders" in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5), refer to a group of psychological disorders characterized by physical symptoms that cannot be fully explained by a general medical condition, substance abuse, or attributable to another mental disorder.

Physical Symptoms: Individuals with somatoform disorders experience significant physical symptoms that may affect various body systems, such as pain, gastrointestinal problems, or neurological symptoms. Lack of Medical Explanation: The symptoms cannot be entirely accounted for by a known medical condition. Despite medical evaluations, there is no clear medical explanation for the severity or extent of the symptoms.

Psychological Factors: Psychological factors, such as stress, anxiety, or unresolved emotional issues, often play a role in the development and maintenance of these physical symptoms.

It's important to note that the term "somatoform disorders" has been replaced by "somatic symptom and related disorders" in the latest edition of the DSM, reflecting a shift in understanding these conditions and their relationship to psychological factors. Treatment often involves a multidisciplinary approach, including psychotherapy, support, and sometimes medication to address associated symptoms like anxiety or depression.

Symptoms

Physical Symptoms

Chronic pain Gastrointestinal problems (such as nausea, bloating, or diarrhea) Sexual symptoms (such as erectile dysfunction or pain during intercourse) Neurological symptoms (such as weakness or loss of sensation) Cardiovascular symptoms (such as palpitations or chest pain) Respiratory symptoms (such as shortness of breath)

Excessive Concern about Symptoms

Excessive worry or preoccupation with the severity of physical symptoms Persistent thoughts about having a serious medical condition.

Medical Help-Seeking Behavior

Frequent medical consultations and excessive use of healthcare resources A pattern of seeking multiple medical opinions, tests, and procedures without relief from symptoms

Disproportionate Distress and Impairment

The level of distress and impairment caused by the symptoms is often disproportionate to the medical findings. The symptoms can significantly interfere with daily functioning, work, and relationships.
